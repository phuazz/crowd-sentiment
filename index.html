How to read this. Green marks the pessimism / buy-side state, red the optimism / trim state — green means the crowd is depressed, not that the market is "good". The composite is a state conditioner, not an entry trigger: historically the S&P 500 compounded at —/ann while the composite sat in pessimism (<40) against —/ann buy-and-hold — pessimism days also run higher volatility, and part of the gap is risk; the edge survives risk adjustment. Optimism (>70) drags for 0–3 months and washes out by 6–12. First touches of a zone are not significant against random-entry nulls; the one exception is a fresh drop below 25, significant at 1–3 months (Signal value tab). The sell side is weaker than the buy side and only bites when financial conditions are tight — treat >70 as trim-and-tighten, never short. Green shading in the price panel marks breadth thrusts — 10-day advancing/declining issues exceeding 1.9, held ~3 months. Thrusts are a one-sided event: a surge of broad buying that historically marks durable bottoms, with no symmetric bearish counterpart — hence no red shading. In extended bears the pessimism state loses money in absolute terms — see Method & caveats.

Financial conditions — is money easy or tight?

What it is. A gauge of stress in the financial system, from the US Office of Financial Research's Financial Stress Index, standardised against its own last three years. Below zero = easy / accommodative money (credit flowing, low stress); above zero = tight / stressed. It is not a sentiment input — it adjusts how much to trust the sentiment signal.

Why it matters here. Euphoria is dangerous mainly when money is tight. When conditions are easy, an over-optimistic crowd has historically kept melting up, so the tool only acts on the sell side when conditions are tight. The table below is why.

Breadth thrust — is broad buying surging?

What it is. A measure of how broadly stocks are rising: advancing versus declining issues over the last 10 days. When that ratio jumps above 1.9 — far more stocks up than down, all at once — a "thrust" fires (▼ markers below).

The green shading, and why the bands differ in length. Each fire is "held" for ~3 months (63 trading days) — the window over which a thrust historically kept mattering. A single, isolated fire therefore shades exactly ~3 months. But at strong bottoms thrusts often fire repeatedly; when fires land within 3 months of each other, their windows overlap and merge into one longer band. So a long green band is not one giant thrust — it is a cluster of them (e.g. mid-2022→early-2023 was five fires chaining into ~9 months), which is a stronger, more sustained signal than a lone fire. One-sided and bullish only — hence no red shading.

Why it matters. Thrusts have historically marked durable bottoms — buying wide enough to signal a real turn, not a dead-cat bounce. The record below shows how the S&P did after each fire since 1995.

Mechanics

Adaptive extreme bands. Each input is smoothed and scored against rolling bands fitted to its own trailing history, so "extreme" adapts across bull and bear markets instead of sitting at fixed levels. A component reads optimism above its upper band and pessimism below its lower band.

Aggregation. The composite is the share of components at bullish extremes, with half-credit for neutral (optimism 100, neutral 50, pessimism 0, averaged over active components — minimum 7 of 12). Weekly series enter at their public release dates, never at their survey dates, and all rolling statistics use only data available on the day — no look-ahead by construction.

Display. The headline line smooths the composite 5 days and uses deep zones at 40/70, chosen from a pre-registered grid (Method tab). The unsmoothed composite is shown faintly; the 5-day smoothing exists to cut zone flips (55 → 15 per year), not as an edge claim.

Why 40 / 70 and 5-day smoothing

deeper zones lift quality at every smoothing; smoothing cuts turnover — matched/continuous variants in the table below
  • Deeper zones (40/70) raise the quality of every read. Versus 45/65, the pessimism-minus-optimism 3-month spread improves in BOTH the pre-2016 and post-2016 eras, at the price of rarer signals (~17% of days in pessimism instead of ~25%).
  • Smoothing is a turnover device, not an edge device. 5 days cuts zone flips from 55 to 15 a year with no loss of test-era spread — but its pre-2016 margin is statistically zero, so it is packaging. Heavier smoothing (10–21d) keeps cutting turnover while lagging the 6-month reads.
  • The choice was made by a frozen rule, committed before results (train AND test spread bars, sign checks, a flips ceiling; ties to fewest flips). 2 of 13 challengers qualified; this cell won the tie-break.
  • These parameters tune the lens, not a strategy. Risk metrics for actually trading the zones — Sharpe, drawdown, bear windows — are in the overlay card on the Signal value tab, where the tested ±20pp tilt failed its pre-registered bar and was not adopted.

Caveats — read before acting on anything here

  • Extended bears: the pessimism state loses money in ABSOLUTE terms. Mean 3m forward from pessimism days — . The conditioning is only relative to those windows' own worse baselines; adding equity on this signal in a downtrend earns the absolute number.
  • State conditioner, not trigger. Zone-entry timing fails random-entry nulls; the deep washout entry (<25) is the one exception (1m p=0.006 / 3m p=0.013 under a cluster-preserving null). Day-level tables use overlapping forward windows — episodes cluster in bear markets and effective sample sizes are far below the day counts shown.
  • Sell side is structurally weaker. Optimism drags 0–3 months, washes out by 6–12, and only bites when financial conditions are tight (see the gate card). Never short on this signal.
  • The smoothing choice has no untouched holdout. The adoption rule consumed the post-2015 window across 13 challengers and the train-side margin is statistically indistinguishable from zero — the 40/70 zone lines carry the evidence; 5-day smoothing is turnover packaging. A costed ±20pp tilt overlay on the signal failed its pre-registered bar and was NOT adopted (Signal value tab).
  • Data quality. Some inputs are modern stand-ins with limited history (leveraged-ETF flows from 2006); the earnings-yield leg uses single-source index-level EPS with a three-month lag; weekly feeds can go stale (components auto-drop after 15 business days and the count is shown above).
  • Sample is one secular-bull era (1995–2026, brief bears). Washout-buying in a 1970s-style regime is untested. No transaction costs, taxes or slippage anywhere on this page except the overlay card. Educational content, not investment advice.
